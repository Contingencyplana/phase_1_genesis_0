# Scene: scene_0704

## Title
A Harbor Argument

## Location
Between Harborfront and Market Quarter

---

## Scene Text

The argument starts as a raised voice and turns into a ring of listeners in less than a minute.

The Wharf Clerk stands with a slate in one hand while a trader points toward a stalled cart and demands unloading priority.

"My goods spoil if they sit," the trader says. "You posted the warning late and shifted everyone after dawn."

A traveler with wet cuffs cuts in, "I missed my transfer because your board changed twice before noon."

One dock worker, still breathing hard from a carry run, jerks a thumb toward the quay.

"You want order? Then stop treating every crate like it's the only crate in the harbor."

Nobody here is arguing theory.

They're arguing time, loss, and who gets pushed back when the lane tightens.

The Wharf Clerk rubs his brow and says, "If you want to contest sequence, do it at the notices where it's written, not in the middle of a carry path."

The crowd shifts, split between following him and drifting back to work.
