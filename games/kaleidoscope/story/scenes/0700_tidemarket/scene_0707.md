# Scene: scene_0707

## Title
View Toward Narrowwater

## Location
Overlook toward the Inner Channels

---

## Scene Text

From the overlook, the harbor's noise drops to a dull hum and the inner channels spread out in narrow bends between dark stone and shoal water.

At this distance, you can see why so many schedules fail before they start.

Only one or two passages look deep enough at the present tide, and even those twist hard before opening again.

A broad-shouldered official in a weather-marked coat studies the channel flags through a brass tube.

When someone calls him Marshal, he answers without turning: "Ferry Marshal, if we're being formal, and formal keeps people from guessing their way into mud."

He lowers the tube and gestures toward a bottleneck where two wakes cross.

"Everything backs there. If Narrowwater slows, Tidemarket slows. People curse this harbor, but half the delay starts beyond sight."

The water network is not background here.

It is the hidden structure under public life.
