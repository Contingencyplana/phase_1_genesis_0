# Scene: scene_0204

## Title
The Visionary's Journal

## Location
Island Library

---

## Scene Text

You step closer to the large wooden table in the center of the library.

Several journals lie open across its surface, their pages yellowed with age. One of them appears to have been left open deliberately, as though someone expected the next visitor to read it.

You gently turn the page.

The handwriting is neat and careful, written in dark ink that has faded slightly with time.

*"For many years this island has been known only as a harbor — a safe refuge for ships crossing the long ocean routes. But I believe Harbor Island is more than a place of rest."*

You turn another page.

Sketches fill the margins. Rough drawings of the island’s coastline, the surrounding forest, and several strange markings deeper inland.

*"There are patterns here that others have not noticed. The currents, the stars, the way certain stones appear beneath the forest soil. All of it suggests intention."*

Another passage has been underlined twice.

*"If my calculations are correct, the island may be part of something far older than the village itself."*

You begin to suspect the same strange symbol may appear somewhere in the island forest.

The next few pages describe expeditions into the forest and long nights spent studying maps of the surrounding seas.

But the final entry ends abruptly.

*"Tomorrow I will travel inland once more. I believe I have finally located the place the old charts describe."*

The page ends there.

No further entries follow.

The journal simply stops.

You look up from the table.

The quiet library suddenly feels heavier with unanswered questions.

Where did the Visionary go?

And what did they find in the forest?
