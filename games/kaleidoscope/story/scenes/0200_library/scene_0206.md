# Scene: scene_0206

## Title
The Old Shelves

## Location
Island Library

---

## Scene Text

You move away from the central table and begin walking slowly along the tall wooden shelves that line the walls of the library.

Most of the books here are old — some very old. Their leather bindings have faded with time, and many titles have worn away completely.

You run a finger along one row of spines.

Navigation logs.  
Fishing records.  
Weather journals.  
Tide charts.

For generations, sailors and villagers have left pieces of their knowledge here.

But deeper in the shelves you begin to notice something unusual.

A small section of books is arranged differently from the others.

These volumes are thinner, more like personal journals than official records. Each one bears the same small symbol pressed faintly into the cover: a circle surrounding a single dot.

The same symbol you noticed on the map.

You pull one of the journals from the shelf.

Inside are sketches of strange stone formations found across the island — tall pillars half-buried in the forest soil, rings of smooth stones hidden beneath moss, and markings carved into rock that resemble the symbol on the cover.

A note appears in the margin beside one sketch.

*"The stones are older than the village."*

Another page contains a brief entry written in the same careful handwriting as the open journal on the table.

*"The island was not chosen at random."*

You close the journal slowly.

The library no longer feels like a simple collection of old books.

It feels like a record of someone trying to understand something far older than the harbor village.

Outside, the wind shifts softly across the hill.
