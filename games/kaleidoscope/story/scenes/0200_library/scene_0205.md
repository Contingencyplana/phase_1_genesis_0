# Scene: scene_0205

## Title
The Island Maps

## Location
Island Library

---

## Scene Text

Several maps are spread across the large wooden table beside the open journal.

You lean closer to study them.

Most appear to be careful surveys of Harbor Island and the surrounding waters. Coastlines have been traced in dark ink, and small handwritten notes mark reefs, currents, and safe anchorages.

But a few of the maps are different.

One shows the forest in unusual detail. Instead of simple paths, faint circles and lines have been drawn deep within the trees, forming shapes that seem almost deliberate.

Another map shows the island surrounded by strange markings in the ocean — arrows, numbers, and symbols that resemble calculations rather than navigation notes.

In the corner of one map, a small annotation has been written in the same precise handwriting as the journal.

*"The island lies at the intersection."*

Below it, another line:

*"The currents, the stars, and the stone formations all agree."*

Your eyes move across the page again.

The markings in the forest seem to form a rough triangle.

One point lies somewhere near the harbor village.

Another appears deeper within the forest.

The final point is marked only with a small symbol — a circle surrounding a dot.

No location name accompanies it.

Just a question mark.

You sit back slightly.

If the Visionary believed these maps were important, then the island may contain places no one in the village has fully explored.

The quiet library seems to hold its breath around you.
