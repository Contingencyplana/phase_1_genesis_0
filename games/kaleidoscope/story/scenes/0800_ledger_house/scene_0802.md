# Scene: scene_0802

## Title
Intake Desk for Contested Sequence

## Location
Ledger House / Claims Intake Desk

---

## Scene Text

A narrow desk runs along the inside wall beneath shelves of tied claim packets.

Names, cargo marks, lane numbers, and timing notes are written in a steady hand across ruled sheets already darkened with thumbprints.

Two traders wait with sealed slips while a dock runner reads a sequence stamp aloud and grimaces.

A clerk gestures them forward one at a time.

"If you contest unloading order," she says, "you file the claim, attach witnesses if you have them, and accept deferred sequence until review."

One trader objects that delay means spoilage.

The clerk nods once.

"Write that too. Loss matters. But shouting in the carry path does not change sequence. Written claims do."

She stacks the slips by lane, then by tide window, then adds a small red mark to one packet and passes it to a runner headed toward the route table.

Inside Ledger House, dispute is not ignored.

It is converted into a format the harbor can act on.
