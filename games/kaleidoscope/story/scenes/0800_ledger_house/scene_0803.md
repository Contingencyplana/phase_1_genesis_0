# Scene: scene_0803

## Title
Route Amendments at the Archive Table

## Location
Ledger House / Route Amendments and Archive Table

---

## Scene Text

The archive table is broad enough for three open ledgers and a row of damp correction slips waiting for sand to dry the ink.

A clerk compares channel notes from Narrowwater with weather amendments delivered from the quay.

"Outer line flagged late," she says to a runner. "Mark the noon window corrected and move the prior copy to revision."

Another hand enters a deferred unloading claim from the intake desk, linking it to a lane code and tide mark so the next board posting will reflect the change.

Nothing here feels ceremonial.

It is precise, repetitive, and quietly decisive.

Route slips, weather corrections, and contest notes are reconciled line by line until rumor becomes durable record.

By the time the updated bundle is sealed for the notice board, the argument that started on the harborfront has become institutional memory.
