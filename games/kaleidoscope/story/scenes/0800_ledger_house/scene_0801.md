# Scene: scene_0801

## Title
Ledger House

## Location
Ledger House / Inland Records Hall

---

## Scene Text

The Ledger House stands just inland from the quay, built in plain timber and stone that favor durability over display.

Inside, long tables hold bound route books, cargo tallies, dispute copies, and weather amendments filed by date, lane, and hand.

Clerks move steadily between shelves and desks, exchanging short phrases that sound more like procedure than conversation.

One clerk taps a stack of tied folios.

"Board notices are the short form," she says. "This is the full record. Delays, corrections, and claims all end up here."

A runner arrives with a damp packet, gets it stamped, and is sent back toward the harbor before the wax has fully set.

The room feels less dramatic than the waterfront and more consequential.

In Tidemarket, this is where argument becomes record.
