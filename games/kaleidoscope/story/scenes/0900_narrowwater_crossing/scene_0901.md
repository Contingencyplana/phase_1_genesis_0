# Scene: scene_0901

## Title
The Ferry Marshal on Narrowwater

## Location
Overlook toward the Inner Channels

---

## Scene Text

The Ferry Marshal does not lower the brass tube when you ask.

He keeps his eye on the channel flags for another moment, then lowers it slowly and turns to face you.

"Narrowwater is not one passage," he says. "It is four, depending on tide and wind, and only two of them are safe at any given time."

He points with the tube toward a bend where the water darkens and the current visibly eddies.

"When the morning tide runs hard, the inner cut closes to anything wider than a flat barge. When the afternoon wind backs west, the outer line fouls with drift. So every boat waits for its window, and every window that slips brings the next one with it."

He folds the tube under his arm.

"One delay here becomes three delays by evening. Three become six by morning. People in the market blame the clerks. Clerks blame the captains. Captains blame the tide."

He gives you a measured look.

"The tide does not care."
