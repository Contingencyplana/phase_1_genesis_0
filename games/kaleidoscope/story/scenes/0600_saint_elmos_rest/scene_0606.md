## Title
The Shrine Niche

## Location
Shrine Niche

## Scene Text

The wind path bends around a stone outcrop and opens onto a shallow niche cut into the rock face.

Inside sit small offerings left by passing crews: shells polished white, knotted twine, worn coins, sea glass, and a brass button gone dull with salt.

Someone has tucked a folded note under a smooth stone, too wet to read now.

The caretaker steps up beside you, hands in their coat pockets.

"People leave a bit of something before rough water," they say. "Out of respect, habit, caution. Depends who you ask."

They glance at the tokens, then back toward the sea.

"Either way, it reminds them to pay attention. That's never wasted."
