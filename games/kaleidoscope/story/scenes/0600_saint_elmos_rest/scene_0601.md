# Scene: scene_0601

## Title
Crossing Toward Saint Elmo's Rest

## Location
Open Water

---

## Scene Text

The old captain studies the horizon for a long moment before he casts off.

"Saint Elmo's Rest isn't a place people sail to for pleasure," he says as the boat clears Harbor Island's shelter. "It's where you put in when weather decides the crossing for you."

Outside the harbor lee, the sea darkens to iron-blue and the swells run steeper than the Brinehook channel.

The captain keeps his voice even, hands steady on the line.

"No town there. No market. Just a jetty, a house, and enough dry boards to wait out a bad turn."

Spray stings your face. Ahead, through low moving cloud, you catch a hard outline of posts and rock.

"You'll never hear anyone boast about reaching it," he says. "But you'll hear gratitude."
