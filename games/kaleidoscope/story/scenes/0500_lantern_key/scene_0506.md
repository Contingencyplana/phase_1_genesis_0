## Title
From the Lantern Tower

## Location

## Scene Text

From the tower landing, the archipelago opens in long bands of water and stone.

Harbor Island sits broader to one side. Brinehook lies lower and quieter in another direction. Between them, narrow channels shine and darken as cloud shadow moves across the sea.

You can see why a lantern matters here.

Routes cross, weather turns quickly, and distance is easy to misjudge from deck level.

Signal poles stand beside the tower with marked arms for storm warnings and lane closures. Their paint is chipped from years of use.

This height does not feel mystical.

It feels responsible.
