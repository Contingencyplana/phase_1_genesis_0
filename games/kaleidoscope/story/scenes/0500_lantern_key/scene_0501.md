# Scene: scene_0501

## Title
Crossing to Lantern Key

## Location
Open Water

---

## Scene Text

The old captain waits for a cleaner break in the chop before casting off.

Outside Harbor Island's lee, the water turns sharper. The sail snaps harder in the wind, and spray blows cold across the bow.

"Keep your knees loose," he says over the rush of water. "This stretch likes to throw people around."

He nods toward a low shape ahead, where a narrow tower rises above dark rock.

"Lantern Key," he says. "Small place, but important. They keep the beacon lit, watch the weather, and pass word when lanes get rough."

A gull vanishes into the gusts.

"Half these islands run because someone keeps watch where the wind is worst."

The Key grows clearer with each swell: working pier, stacked casks, and bright paint worn thin by salt.
