## Title
The Keeper

## Location

## Scene Text

The keeper sets down a wrench and gives you a short nod.

"You're with the old captain," they say, glancing toward the pier. "Good. He reads the water better than most."

They point to a slate covered in times and wind notes.

"We keep the light clean, log the weather, and send warning flags when channels turn bad. If our signal misses, somebody else pays for it."

Their voice is steady, matter-of-fact.

"Some nights you see lights out where no boat should be. Usually it's weather bending distance. Sometimes it's someone late and lucky."

They cinch a knot on a line and look up the tower path.

"Either way, you keep the lantern ready. That's the work."
