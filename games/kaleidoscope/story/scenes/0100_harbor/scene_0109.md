# Scene: scene_0109

## Title
Exploring Harbor Island

## Location
Harbor Market

---

## Scene Text

The fisherman scratches his beard thoughtfully when you ask about the strange things people have seen around the island.

"Harbor Island looks small from the sea," he says, "but islands have a way of hiding their secrets."

He gestures loosely around the harbor.

"Most folks stay near the village. Fish the waters, trade a little, and keep to what they know."

He points toward the hill where the old stone library stands.

"But that place attracts curious minds. Travelers go up there looking for answers in those dusty journals."

Then he nods toward the distant tree line beyond the village.

"And the forest… well, that's where the old stories live."

The other fisherman chuckles quietly.

"Ruins, strange lights, paths that don't always lead where you expect. At least, that's what the travelers claim."

The first fisherman shrugs.

"Truth is, nobody here has explored the whole island. Not properly."

He gives you a measuring look.

"But travelers like you usually try."

Around you the market carries on as usual — merchants calling out, gulls circling overhead, and the steady rhythm of the harbor.

Harbor Island suddenly feels a little larger than it did when you first arrived.
