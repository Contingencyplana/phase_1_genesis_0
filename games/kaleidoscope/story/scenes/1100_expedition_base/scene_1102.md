## Title
The Guide

## Location
Inland Archives / Route Table

---

## Scene Text

The guide has the look of someone who trusts paths more than stories but has learned that stories often cling to paths anyway.

He keeps one hand on the table while the other moves between a ridge sketch, a tide-copied ledger note, and a narrow strip of bark marked with older trail turns.

"Most people think inland travel begins where the boats stop," he says without looking up. "It doesn't. It begins where the records stop agreeing with each other."

He taps three points on three different papers.

One is a delivery notation from Tidemarket.
One is an older survey copy with no current route name.
One is a charcoal rubbing filed years ago as if it were a curiosity rather than a guide.

"Same rise," he says. "Different hands. Different years. Same place."

There is nothing mystical in his voice.

Only the steady confidence of someone who has learned that old marks and practical routes sometimes describe the same ground from opposite ends.

"If you want the interior," he says, "you do not start by wandering. You start by noticing what people kept writing down without understanding why it matched."
