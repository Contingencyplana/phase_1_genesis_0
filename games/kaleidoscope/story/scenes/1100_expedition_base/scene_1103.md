# Scene: scene_1103

## Title
Maps and Contradiction

## Location
Inland Archives / Comparison Table

---

## Scene Text

Spread side by side, the papers disagree at first glance.

A clean civic route copy marks a carrier path that ends at a storage rise inland.
An older hand-drawn sheet shows a line continuing past that point into country left blank by later record-keepers.
One of the copied Visionary notes uses no route name at all, only a margin mark and a brief line about "the inward continuation the harbor records mistake for an end."

Taken alone, each document could be dismissed.

Together, they begin to press against dismissal.

The contradiction is not that one map is wrong and another right.

It is that practical records, older survey traces, and the Visionary's partial notation all seem to be touching the same hidden route from different directions.

The room remains quiet around you: paper, wood, measured ink, and the soft scrape of someone moving a weight along the edge of a chart.

This is not revelation.

It is stronger than rumor and smaller than proof.

It is the first time the archipelago's stored knowledge feels as if it is leaning inland.
