## Title
Inland Archives

## Location
Inland Archives / Expedition Room

## Scene Text

The room sits above the damp line of the quay and well below the grandness that the word archive might suggest.

It is a long workroom of shelves, map cases, tied packets, and tables scarred by compass points and straightedge cuts.

Copies from the Ledger House have been carried here and sorted beside older survey leaves, route books, cargo notes, and bundles of rubbings taken from stones no one now visits as often as they once did.

Nothing in the room feels ceremonial.

It feels assembled.

A place made because enough fragments had begun to point in the same direction that someone finally decided they should be kept together.

At one end of the room, a weathered guide is leaning over a spread of route papers while a clerk ties fresh amendment slips into a packet marked for inland carriers.

For the first time, the archipelago's records do not feel like the memory of what has happened.

They feel like preparation for where someone means to go next.
