# Scene: scene_1202

## Title
Route Names That Do Not Match

## Location
Inland Rise Path / Marker Cut

---

## Scene Text

The first inland stretch is steeper than it looked from the harbor records.

At a marker cut above the rise, three route marks share one stone: a current carrier code, an older survey notch pattern, and a weathered scratch locals still use when they give spoken directions.

The names do not match.

The turns do.

A load team pauses long enough to compare your folded copy against the post slate.

One finger traces the civic endpoint.

Another taps a faded continuation line beyond it.

"Same road," one of them says. "Different offices, different years."

The mismatch is not confusion.

It is accumulation.

Paths that stayed useful kept being renamed by whoever had to file them.
