# Kaleidoscope — The Archipelago Eggshell Planet

This document describes the broader planetary structure of the world in which the Kaleidoscope Interactive Novel takes place.

The story begins on **Harbor Island**, a small island within a much larger ocean filled with archipelagos and distant horizons.

Some scholars believe that the world itself may be far stranger than it first appears.

The purpose of this document is to describe the **largest known structure of the world** while preserving the mystery surrounding its true nature.

---

# The World Ocean

Most travelers understand the world primarily through the **World Ocean**.

The World Ocean is a vast ocean filled with countless islands and archipelagos.  
Ships travel between these islands carrying travelers, goods, rumors, and discoveries.

Many regions of the ocean are well known and frequently visited.  
Other regions remain distant, poorly mapped, and rarely explored.

Because of the immense scale of the ocean, no map is believed to capture the entire world.

Sailors often say that every horizon suggests another archipelago waiting to be discovered.

---

# Archipelagos

Islands throughout the World Ocean often appear in groups known as **archipelagos**.

An archipelago may contain:

- many small islands
- several large islands surrounded by smaller ones
- long chains of islands stretching across great distances

Different archipelagos frequently develop their own cultures, traditions, and histories.

Travelers sometimes refer to distant archipelagos as if they were **worlds of their own**, because life there can feel very different from life elsewhere in the ocean.

---

# Island Chains

Within many archipelagos, islands may form smaller regional groupings known as **island chains**.

Island chains often share:

- trade routes
- local traditions
- common harbors
- closely connected communities

Travel between islands within the same chain is often frequent and familiar.

Beyond those chains, however, the wider ocean remains vast and uncertain.

---

# Islands

Individual islands form the smallest geographic unit commonly recognized by travelers.

Islands may vary greatly in size and character.

Some islands contain thriving settlements and busy harbors.  
Others remain quiet, remote, or completely uninhabited.

Every island may hold its own stories, discoveries, and mysteries.

---

# Harbor Island

The Kaleidoscope story begins on **Harbor Island**, one island among many within the World Ocean.

Harbor Island serves as a meeting place for travelers, sailors, scholars, and explorers.

From its harbor, ships depart toward nearby islands and distant archipelagos.

Many of the earliest discoveries in the story begin here.

---

# Rumors of an Eggshell World

Among scholars and sailors there are persistent rumors about the true nature of the world.

Some believe that the planet may be hollow, like an **eggshell**, with oceans flowing across the outer shell and unknown realms existing beneath it.

According to these theories, the surface where travelers live would be only the **outer surface** of a much larger structure.

Others suggest that strange tunnels, deep caverns, or forgotten mechanisms might one day reveal passages to an **Inner Surface** hidden within the world.

Most sailors dismiss these ideas as speculation.

For now, the World Ocean remains the only world most travelers will ever see.

---

# Expansion of the World

The Kaleidoscope world is designed to expand gradually.

New islands, island chains, and archipelagos may appear as the story grows.

Future discoveries might include:

- distant archipelagos beyond the known ocean
- unusual islands shaped by strange ideas or forgotten experiments
- mysterious structures hidden beneath the sea
- evidence supporting — or disproving — the theory of an eggshell world

The true scale of the world may remain unknown for a long time.

---

# World Structure

Scholars sometimes describe the structure of the world in the following way:

```
Eggshell Planet (theoretical)
    ↓
World Ocean
    ↓
Archipelagos
    ↓
Island chains
    ↓
Islands
    ↓
Harbor Island
```

Only the lowest levels of this structure are currently known with certainty.

Everything beyond the World Ocean remains a subject of speculation and exploration.

---

# Design Principle

The Kaleidoscope world should always feel larger than what players can currently see.

Every harbor suggests another island.  
Every island suggests another archipelago.  
And every horizon suggests that the World Ocean continues far beyond the known maps.
